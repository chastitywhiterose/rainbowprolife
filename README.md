# Rainbow Pro-Life Alliance

A collection of historical importance for the Rainbow Pro-Life Alliance. It is an anti-abortion group I am a member of, and I volunteer my skills as a writer.

# Purpose

I am Chastity White Rose, the author of many books on various subjects, and also computer programs, on which I tend to write technical books. I believe in the power of words and numbers. Writing information down so that it is not lost is one of the most important things to me. For this reason, I have started to collect writings from LGBTQIA+ individuals like myself, especially those who work with the Rainbow Pro-Life Alliance now and in the past.

The current official website for RPLA is here:

<https://www.rainbowprolife.org/>

There is no Pro-Life group quite like the Rainbow Pro-Life Alliance. Formerly, we were called the Pro-Life Alliance of Gays and Lesbians (PLAGAL). This group is where people go who wish to fight against the crime of baby murder, but are not welcome because they are Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender, Queer, Intersex, Asexual or Plus (anything other label that people tend to exclude and discriminate against).

My purpose in this archival project is to preserve information on people like me whose voices have been silenced by mainstream Pro-Life groups for not being the "right" denomination of Christianity or perhaps for just being too gay.

# Origin

This project actually started because preserving history was on my mind following the publishing of a book about Assembly language programming for DOS. Old computer operating systems and programming languages tended to teach people more about technology. As I thought about the books I wrote to teach people the software development of the 1980s, I realized how important it was to preserve history in other parts of life.

At a board meeting on 5-28-2026, I brought up the idea for us to publish a paperback book and also a more consistent blog. After all, posts on social media can be censored and deleted because those who profit from abortion don't like it when their business is criticized or stopped by people who oppose murder.

And the board was generally in agreement with me that we need to organize and make available our history. We will discuss more about working together on this. Still, I consider myself the catalyst of this because I have the computer skills and know a lot about how to convert text into PDFs and EPUBs for publishing in various ways.

I have started this collection with some of my best posts that I wrote over the years. Other people are welcome to contribute things they have written related to Pro-Life activism, LGBTQIA+, or both.
